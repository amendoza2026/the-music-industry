Languages Learned

Originally, this was going to be a website about languages, but I scrapped that idea for a Taylor Swift website instead, but in true Taylor Swift fashion, I decided to keep one page, and this is that page.

All but one of these languages I cannot speak fluently. The languages are listed alphabetically because I can't recall in what order I *tried to* learn them.

Dutch

Based purely on how languages evolved, Dutch is the major language that's most closely related to English. I actually can't say much about *how* related these languages are 'cause I barely learned anything from Duolingo (#ProudDuolingoHater). What I can say though is how similar their vocabularies are.

I've seen some TikToks before telling people that turning your phone's language to Dutch would result in funny app names that sound like something the stonks meme would say, like "klok", "kalender", and "telefoon". You see a funny meme, but I see proof of how related Dutch and English are.

Puur gebaseerd op hoe talen zich hebben ontwikkeld, is het Nederlands de belangrijkste taal die het meest verwant is aan het Engels. Ik kan eigenlijk niet veel zeggen over *hoe* deze talen verwant zijn, want ik heb nauwelijks iets van Duolingo geleerd (#TrotseDuolingoHater). Wat ik wel kan zeggen is hoe vergelijkbaar hun woordenschat is.

Ik heb al eens TikToken gezien die mensen vertelden dat als je de taal van je telefoon op Nederlands zet, je grappige app-namen krijgt die klinken als iets wat de stonks meme zou zeggen, zoals "klok", "kalender" en "telefoon". Jij ziet een grappige meme, maar ik zie het bewijs hoe verwant Nederlands en Engels zijn.

Esperanto

Probably the most spoken conlang, or constructed language, I tried learning Esperanto as I was lured in by its "international language" premise. After learning enough of the words, I noticed that it was really only based on Romance languages or languages that sprang from Latin. Maybe if it were branded as a unifying Romance language with Polish influences, then perhaps I would tolerate Esperanto more.

Regardless of that, I will admit that this conlang is undoubtedly the most successful. No conlang has ever had native speakers other than Esperanto (I think).

Verŝajne la plej parolata konstruita lingvo, aŭ konstruita lingvo, mi provis lerni Esperanton ĉar mi estis logita de ĝia "internacia lingvo" premiso. Lerninte sufiĉe da la vortoj, mi rimarkis, ke ĝi vere baziĝas nur sur latinidaj lingvoj aŭ lingvoj, kiuj fontas el la latina. Eble se ĝi estus markita kiel unueciga latinida lingvo kun polaj influoj, tiam eble mi pli tolerus Esperanton.

Sendepende de tio, mi konfesos, ke tiu ĉi konstruita lingvo estas sendube la plej sukcesa. Neniu konstruita lingvo iam havis denaskajn parolantojn krom Esperanto (mi pensas).

French

Oui, oui, baguette. J'aime l'eau de toilette.

Ah, the language of love, making everything sound so sophisticated. French somewhat (by somewhat, I mean 45%) influenced the English vocabulary, so it probably wouldn't be a surprise to find out that learning French vocabulary was kind of a piece of cake.

That was the only part of language learning that I focused on.

Ita, ita, vectis panis. Aquam latrinae amo.

Ah, la langue de l'amour, qui fait tout paraître si sophistiqué. Le français a quelque peu (par quelque peu, je veux dire 45%) influencé le vocabulaire anglais, donc ce ne serait probablement pas une surprise de découvrir qu'apprendre le vocabulaire français était un vrai jeu d'enfant.

C'est la seule partie de l'apprentissage des langues sur laquelle je me suis concentrée.

Japanese

Japanese is the language I most recently studied. I probably studied Japanese because of two reasons: to be able to speak with my Japanese relatives, and to be challenged by the three writing systems of Japanese. Yes, Japanese has three writing systems. To be honest, I actually had the most passion to learn a language in learning Japanese, but I suddenly lost that passion, and I blame that on the ever-challenging kanjis.

If you don't know yet, kanjis are actually just characters from the Chinese language borrowed into Japanese, and I'm hoping you know that Chinese has A LOT of characters. And to add to the mess, Japanese also uses 2 other syllabaries. Yes, 2 syllabaries. One is mainly used for native and Chinese words, and the other is used for loanwords, onomatopoeias, and technical terms.

日本語は一番最近勉強した言語です。日本語を勉強した理由は、日本人の親戚と会話ができるようになるためと、日本語の3つの文字体系に挑戦するためでしょう。そう、日本語には3つの文字があるのです。正直なところ、日本語を学ぶ際に言語を学ぶことに最も情熱を持っていたのですが、突然その情熱を失ってしまい、それを常に挑戦し続ける漢字のせいにしています。

漢字は中国語の文字を日本語に借用したもので、中国語にはたくさんの文字があることをご存知でしょうか。さらに、日本語は2つの音節文字を使用しています。そう、2つの音節文字である。一つは主に母国語や漢語に使われ、もう一つは外来語や擬音語、専門用語に使われる。

Korean

I did not learn Korean because of K-pop or K-dramas. I learned it because it supposedly uses the easiest alphabet to learn. I do agree with that fact thought. I probably learned Hangeul in like an hour or two. What wasn't easy to learn was how those letters are pronounced. I could pronounce most of them easily, but what perplexed me was the difference between g and kk, and other similar pairings.

Did I tell you that this is the first language I studied? Yeah, I tried learning Korean before Spanish. Silly me.

나는 한국 가요나 한국 드라마 때문에 한국어를 배우지 않았다. 아마 가장 배우기 쉬운 알파벳을 사용하기 때문에 배웠어. 나는 그 사실에 동의해. 아마 한 두 시간 만에 한글을 배웠을 거예요. 배우기가 쉽지 않았던 것은 그 글자들이 어떻게 발음되는가 하는 것이었습니다. 나는 대부분 쉽게 발음할 수 있었지만, 나를 당황하게 한 것은 ㄱ과 ㄲ, 그리고 다른 비슷한 쌍의 차이였다.

내가 너에게 이것이 내가 처음으로 공부한 언어라고 말했니? 네, 저는 스페인어보다 한국어를 먼저 배우려고 노력했어요. 말도 안 돼!

Latin

Yes, I tried learning Latin once. I actually learned Latin the most on this list. It was probably because of the learning method I used. Instead of learning Latin through English, I learned it through Latin itself, without any English! I did this by reading a Latin storybook that started with easily understood sentences and then slowly used more complex Latin. The book was "Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata." Wow, advertisement. I'll stop writing about Latin now because translating texts into Latin with just a dictionary is hell.

Sic est, linguam latinam discere quondam conabar. Linguam latinam in hoc indice re vera maxime didici. Probabiliter fuit quod ratio discendi quae usus fui. Quum posset lingua anglica linguam latinam discere, lingua latina ipsa didici, sine ulla lingua anglica! Hunc feci legendo liber latinus qui faciles sententias incipit tum magis multiplices sermones latinas lente utebatur. Hic liber est "Lingua Latina per se Illustrata." Hui, proscriptio. Nunc de lingua latina scribere desinam quod orationes in linguam latinam lexico solum vertere est infernus.

Spanish

Just like the case in Japanese, I also have a relative who can speak Spanish, my great-grandmother. Obviously, I tried learning Spanish to speak with her, even though she can speak Kapampangan and understand Filipino.

This is such a random fact, but this was the first language I learned on Duolingo. No, not Korean. I learned Korean on another app. Another random fact. I once took a Spanish fluency test that only really tested if I could translate Spanish text to English, no listening, no speaking, and no English-to-Spanish translations. Probably because of how similar the vocabulary of Spanish is to English and Filipino, I was supposedly fluent in Spanish.

Al igual que en el caso del japonés, también tengo un pariente que sabe hablar español, mi bisabuela. Obviamente, intenté aprender español para hablar con ella, aunque sabe hablar pampango y entender el filipino.

Es un hecho tan aleatorio, pero éste fue el primer idioma que aprendí en Duolingo. No, no es el coreano. Aprendí coreano en otra aplicación. Otro hecho aleatorio. Una vez hice una prueba de fluidez en español que sólo evaluaba si podía traducir un texto en español al inglés, sin escuchar, ni hablar, ni traducir del inglés al español. Probablemente, debido a la similitud del vocabulario del español con el inglés y el filipino, se suponía que tenía fluidez en español.

Toki Pona

Because I'm the mastermind behind everything, the alphabet naturally saved the best one for last. If you guessed that this language was the one I'm fluent in, then you're correct! Insert face with party horn and party hat emoji.

This is the third conlang in the list, and frankly, it is the simplest conlang. Actually, it is arguably the simplest language with around 137 words and blunt grammar. I admit, Toki Pona is probably the only language in the list I'm fluent in because of how simple it is.

nasin sitelen li awen e nanpa wan lon pini tan mi lawa e ale. sina pilin e ni: mi ken toki e toki ni, la sina lon! o lon e sitelen pilin pi ilo kalama musi pi len lawa musi.

lipu ni la toki ni li toki sin nanpa tu wan. toki sin ante la, toki sin ni li pona. ken la, toki ante la, toki ni li pona tan ni: ona li jo e nimi ale mute luka luka luka tu e nasin pona. mi ken toki e ni: lipu ni la, mi ken toki e toki pona taso tan ona li pona mute.
